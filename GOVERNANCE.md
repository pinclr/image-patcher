# Governance

This document describes how the image-patcher project is governed. It is
intentionally lightweight and will evolve as the community grows.

## Roles

### Contributors

Anyone who contributes code, documentation, issues, reviews, or other help is
a contributor. There is no formal process to become a contributor — just open
an issue or pull request. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md).

### Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for the overall health and direction of the
project: reviewing and merging pull requests, triaging issues, cutting
releases, and upholding the [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

The current maintainers are listed in [MAINTAINERS.md](./MAINTAINERS.md).

## Decision making

We strive for **consensus**. Most decisions are made implicitly through the
normal pull request and issue process: a change is accepted when a maintainer
approves it and there are no unresolved objections from other maintainers.

For larger or contentious decisions (architecture changes, breaking changes,
governance changes), we use **lazy consensus**: a proposal is opened as an
issue or PR, given reasonable time for feedback, and adopted if no maintainer
objects. If consensus cannot be reached, the decision is made by a simple
majority vote of the maintainers.

## Becoming a maintainer

Contributors who have shown sustained, high-quality involvement — meaningful
code or documentation contributions, helpful reviews, and good community
conduct — may be nominated as maintainers by an existing maintainer. The
nomination is adopted if approved by the existing maintainers (lazy consensus,
with a simple-majority fallback).

We actively welcome maintainers from outside the founding organization;
broad, vendor-neutral participation is a goal of the project.

## Stepping down / inactivity

Maintainers may step down at any time by submitting a PR removing themselves
from [MAINTAINERS.md](./MAINTAINERS.md). Maintainers who are inactive for an
extended period may be moved to emeritus status by the remaining maintainers.

## Changes to this document

Changes to governance are themselves proposed via pull request and adopted by
maintainer consensus.
